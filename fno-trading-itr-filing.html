For taxpayers with intraday trading, futures/options, broker reports, trading losses and business income reporting needs.

F&O and intraday activity needs business-income review

A broker profit-and-loss statement does not by itself decide return-form selection, turnover, admissible expenses, audit applicability or the treatment of losses. The full trading activity and other income sources must be reviewed together.

Records commonly needed

  • Segment-wise broker P&L and transaction statement
  • F&O, intraday and delivery activity separately identified
  • Turnover working and loss details
  • Trading-related expense records and bank entries
  • Opening losses and prior return acknowledgements
  • Salary, interest, capital gains and other income information

Loss and compliance points

The nature of the loss, filing date and return particulars can affect whether set-off or carry-forward is available. Books or audit questions cannot be answered only from the profit figure; they depend on turnover, income method and other statutory facts.

Professional note: Do not infer audit applicability or loss eligibility from a social-media shortcut. The conclusion depends on complete records and current law.
