NRI status is a tax conclusion, not only a label

Citizenship, visa status and bank-account type do not by themselves determine Indian income-tax residential status. Day counts, past presence and the facts of the relevant financial year should be established first.

Information commonly reviewed

  • India travel and stay details for the relevant years
  • Salary, rent, interest and capital gains arising in India
  • NRO/NRE account interest and withholding records
  • Property and investment transactions
  • Foreign income or asset-reporting questions where applicable
  • Foreign tax credit or treaty documents where relevant

Form selection and reporting scope

Residential status can affect the scope of income and disclosure obligations. The correct form also depends on capital gains, business income, assets, losses and other facts; a return should not be selected only because the taxpayer lives abroad.

Professional note: Cross-border reporting and treaty positions depend on documents, dates and the applicable law in both jurisdictions. No treaty benefit or refund is guaranteed.
