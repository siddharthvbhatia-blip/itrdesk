AIS / 26AS mismatch review

For taxpayers whose Form 16, AIS, Form 26AS, bank interest or investment details are not matching before filing.

Start WhatsApp enquiryView checklist

AIS and Form 26AS serve different review purposes

AIS is an information statement that can help identify reported financial activity. Form 26AS is important for tax credits and specified information. Neither should be copied into a return without checking Form 16/16A, bank statements, broker reports and the taxpayer's own records.

Common mismatch patterns

  • Salary or TDS appearing under an incorrect period or amount
  • Bank interest not included in the initial computation
  • Duplicate, incomplete or unfamiliar investment transactions
  • Capital-gain information without correct acquisition cost
  • Tax deducted but credit not matching the taxpayer record
  • Information belonging to another income source or capacity

A practical reconciliation sequence

  1. Download the relevant statements from the official portal.
  2. Match each material item to the source document.
  3. Identify missing income, duplicate information and tax-credit differences.
  4. Use the available feedback or correction route where appropriate.
  5. Prepare the return from the supported final position.
Professional note: Submitting AIS feedback does not by itself complete return reporting or guarantee correction. Preserve supporting records for the position taken.
