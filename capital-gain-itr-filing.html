Capital gain ITR filing

For investors with shares, mutual funds, property sale or other capital gain transactions needing careful ITR reporting.

Capital-gain reporting starts with transaction detail

Shares, mutual funds, property and other capital assets can involve different holding periods, tax rates and reporting schedules. A broker or registrar report is useful, but it should be checked against contract notes, purchase history, corporate actions and the taxpayer's records.

Information commonly reviewed

  • Sale and acquisition dates, values and expenses
  • Short-term or long-term classification
  • Broker and mutual-fund capital-gain statements
  • Bonus, split, merger or other corporate-action history
  • Property purchase/sale documents and eligible costs
  • AIS entries, losses and any exemption claim documents

Why AIS alone is not the computation

AIS can help identify transactions but may not establish the correct cost, holding period or final taxable amount. Differences should be reconciled before the return is prepared. Capital gains also commonly mean that ITR-1 is not the correct form.

Professional note: Classification, cost and exemption treatment depend on the asset, dates, documents and applicable law.
