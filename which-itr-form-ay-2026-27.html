AY 2026-27 form-selection guide

Which income-tax return form may apply?

ITR form selection cannot safely be based only on income amount. Residential status, each income source, directorship, unlisted shares, foreign assets, losses and business facts can change the answer.

Ask about your case
Important: This is a screening guide, not an automatic form selector. Always check the notified AY 2026-27 form, instructions and your complete facts before filing.

Start with the taxpayer and every income source

Before choosing a form, list salary or pension, every house property, bank and other interest, dividends, capital gains, virtual digital assets, lottery or other special-rate income, business or professional income, partnership-firm receipts, foreign income and agricultural income. Then review residential status and the additional disclosure conditions.

Common form-selection traps

Salary does not automatically mean ITR-1

A salaried taxpayer may also have share sales, mutual-fund redemptions, two house properties, foreign shares or a non-resident status. These facts can change the form even if salary is the largest income.

F&O and intraday are not ordinary capital gains

Futures and options and intraday trading are generally examined under business-income principles. Broker statements, turnover, expenses, losses, books and possible audit implications require separate review.

Presumptive taxation is not automatic

Turnover, profession type, mode of receipt, declared profit, loss position and statutory conditions must be reviewed before treating a case as presumptive and selecting ITR-4.

NRI and foreign-asset facts need separate attention

Residential status is determined under the applicable law, not merely citizenship or the location of a bank account. Foreign income and asset disclosures can materially affect the return and form.

Documents that help confirm the form

  • Form 16, AIS and Form 26AS
  • All bank-interest and dividend details
  • Broker capital-gain and trading reports
  • House-property ownership, rent and loan details
  • Business/professional income and expense records
  • Residential-status travel details and foreign-income/asset information, where relevant
  • Previous return and carried-forward loss information

Frequently asked questions

Can a salaried taxpayer always file ITR-1?

No. Other income and disclosure facts can make ITR-1 unavailable.

Which form generally covers F&O and intraday?

ITR-3 is commonly relevant for an individual or HUF because these activities are generally examined under business-income reporting. Complete facts must still be reviewed.

Is ITR-4 the form for every small business?

No. It is restricted to eligible presumptive-income cases and remains subject to the notified conditions and exclusions.
